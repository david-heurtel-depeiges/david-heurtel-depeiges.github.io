## Published papers

| Title / Conference | Authors and short summary | Link |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Listening to the Noise: Blind Denoising with Gibbs Diffusion / **ICML 2024** |  Generalizing the results from *Removing Dust from CMB Observations with Diffusion Models*, we integrate the component separation method in a more general bayesian pipeline, introducing a new method for cosmological inference (proof of concept). In addition, this sampling algorithm allows for blind denoising of images under parametrized **colored** Gaussian noise. Joint work with [Charles Margossian](https://charlesm93.github.io/), [Ruben Ohana](https://rubenohana.github.io/) and [Bruno Regaldo-Saint Blancard](https://users.flatironinstitute.org/~bregaldosaintblancard/)| [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.19455) |
| --- | --- | --- |
|*Removing Dust from CMB Observations with Diffusion Models* / **NeurIPS2023 ML4PS Workshop (Oral)**| In this paper with [Blakesley Burkhart](https://www.bburkhart.com/), [Ruben Ohana](https://rubenohana.github.io/) and [Bruno Regaldo-Saint Blancard](https://users.flatironinstitute.org/~bregaldosaintblancard/), we present a proof of concept for refining the modeling of the Galactic dust foreground, a pivotal aspect of cosmic microwave background (CMB) observations. Employing diffusion models trained on simulated dust emission maps, we demonstrate their potential in component separation of mixed signals of dust and CMB. We show that key summary statistics of said fields are well recovered by this process, paving the way for cosmological inference.| [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.16285) |
